Important Figures of the Civil War

Robert E. Lee

Robert E.Lee was born on January 19th 1807 and came from a prestigious family, his father being a veteran. In 1825, Lee began his military career at west point where he would soon distinguish himself. After this he served in the Mexican-American war, in which he earned accolades for his outstanding service. However, Lee is most famous for his involvement in the cival war. Initially he was offered to be the commander of all Union armies but due to his loyalty to his home state, Virginia, he sided with the Confederacy when Virginia seceded. After taking command of the Army of Northern Virginia in 1862, Lee began to gain fame across the United States as he won victory after victory with his outnumbered force. After the defeat at Gettysburg and the brutal Overland Campaign Lee eventually surrended his army effectively ending the war. Once the war was over, Lee recieved a pardon for his crimes against the nation and spent the rest of his life promiting unity and reconciliation between the 2 sides of the civil war. He eventually died at the age of 63 on October 12th, 1870. Learn more about this figure here.

Ulysses S. Grant

Ulysses S. Grant was born on April 27, 1822 and was born and raised in Ohio as an average citizen. He joined West Point and fought in the Mexican American War but resigned from the army in 1854. When the civil war broke out, he rejoined the army and fought in the Western theater. He quickly rose to prominance after his victories at Fort Donelson and the Battle of Shiloh. He continued to fight in the Western theater until he fought the masterful battle of Vicksburg, after which he was promoted to Lieutenant General and took command of the Army of the Potomac. In this position he managed to finnaly defeat Robert E. Lee and end the Civil War. After the war Grant managed to secure a pardon for all soldiers who fought for the Confederacy helping speed up reconciliation. In 1868 he ran and won the presidential election, becoming president of the USA for the next 8 years and was president during the period of reconstruction after the civil war. After his presidency he went on a world tour, started multiple failed business, and failed to run for a third term. Eventually, he retired and then died at the age of 63 on July 23rd 1885. Abraham Lincoln

Abraham Lincoln was born on Febuary 12th, 1809 and was born into poverty. He grew up in Indiana till he moved to Illinois where he was a self educated lawyer. He entered politics in 1854 after the Kansas-Nebraska act allowed slavery in more states. He quickly became leader of the Republican party and campaigned on a platform of anti-slavery. After he was elected Southern states saw his presidency as a threat and began to secede from the USA. In 1863 he issued the Emancipation Proclamation which ended slavery in the United States, and to this day is one of his most famous deeds. Lincoln also gave many inspirational speechs throughout his career including the famous "Gettysburg Adress" in which he said "and that government of the people, by the people, for the people, shall not perish from the earth." Once the war concluded he did not live long to see the future of the nation. On April 14th, 1865 John Wilkes Booth assasinated Lincoln, permentantly changing the course of history.
